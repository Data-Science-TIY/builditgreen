Energy Analysis

New York City is the first city to mandate the release of energy use data for commercial and residential buildings. Our study matched LEED buildings to NYC block and lot numbers using New Yorkâ€™s Geoclient API and hand checked to assure that matches were correct.

Our analysis focuses on multifamily housing space. We found twenty multifamily new construction projects certified by LEED before 2013, when the most recent energy use data is available. As more buildings are certified and recent energy usage comes online we hope to expand our analysis.

Two measurements are used in our analysis - total greenhouse gas emissions and Temperature Weighted Source EUI. Source energy represents the total amount of raw fuel that is required to operate the building. It incorporates all transmission, delivery, and production losses. Total greenhouse gas aggregates different greenhouse emissions into a single measure, using global warming potentials.

Score Distribution

Interesting Findings: Most of these LEED buildings were scored on the 2.1 and 2.2 LEED NC versions. They averaged a LEED score of 37, with the most common score being a 33, right at the cutoff for Silver Certification. As we have seen in other analyses, the credits that are most heavily tied to building energy performance are not generally achieved until LEED status reaches Gold and Platinum.

Further analysis: How do the individual buildings do against buildings of a similar size and use in New York City? How expensive is LEED certification? How do builders decide which level to pursue?
