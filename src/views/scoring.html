LEED Credits

Trends in LEED credit completion vary by year and level of achievement (i.e. silver, gold, platinum). Individual credits are broken down to visualize which credits are the most and least common achieved. Credits related to low emitting materials and reducing water consumption appear to become industry standards. While credits like innovative waste water technologies are almost never achieved. From version 2.1 to 2.2 the overall trends in points achieved are positive. However, from version 2.2 to 2.9 the trend is not as positive. The scoring system changed from 69 points to 110 possible points. Some points became harder to achieve while others appear easier.

Interesting Findings: Optimizing energy and on-site reneable energy are hard to do. Projects aiming for Platinum are more likely to optimize energy performance (EAc1) and include on-site renewable energy (EAc2). The difference between achievement is less for enhanced refrigerant (EAc4), a credit that appears easier to meet.

Further analysis: In what states and cities are the platinum projects clustered? Does geography influence score? What qualities of the project (e.g. building type, geographic location, population density) are correlated with higher scores?

Interesting Findings: Some of these credits appear easy to meet and may now be indsutry standards. For example, almost all projects meet indoor environmental quality plan during construction (EQc3.1) and low emitting materials (EQc4.1, EQc4.2, EQc4.3).

Further analysis: Has performance on these credits increased compared to prior versions?

Interesting Findings: Platinum is less significant with regional priorty credits. Almost all projects complete IDc2, which promotes using a LEED certified professional on the project.

Further analysis: Regional Priority changes by geographic region. What is the best performing region?

Interesting Findings: Material and Resources credits are the hardest to achieve. MRc1.2 - Building Reuse (Maintain Existing Interior Nonstructural Elements), MRc3 - Materials Reuse, and MRc6 Rapidly Renewable Materials are almost never completed.

Further analysis: What projects are able to achieve certification on these points that are hardest to achieve? Are there cost factors that make these credits unlikely?

The low scores for SSc3 and SSc5.1 are expected because not all projects are located on a brownfield site or include wildlife habitat to restore. Is it realistic to include this as a goal for all projects?

Further analysis: Alternative transportation is easier to achieve in urban areas and stormwater design is more important in urban areas. How are the rural projects performing on alternative transportation? How are the urban projects performing on stormwater design?

Interesting Findings: Technologies like low flow sinks and toliets that help meet WEc3 are likely more readily available and easier to implement than innovative waster water technologies (WEc2).

Further analysis: Are there some states or regions that implement waaste water technologies more than others? And are regulations or building codes a reason for any geographic underperformance?

Interesting Findings: Overall performance on the public scored projects has declined from 57.25 to 54.45 since 2011. Much of this underperformance can be traced to a decline in the average scores for EAc1 and EAc2.

Further analysis: Has the ratio of Platinum, Gold, Silver and Certified projects changed? If so, what is the driver of the shift to lower LEED certified status? If not, why are scores declining overall?

Interesting Findings: Scores in EAc1 - Optimize Energy Performance have declined, on average, from 9.49 to 7.96 (out of 19 possible) and scores on EAc2 - On-site Renewable Energy have declined from 1.70 to .87 (out of 7 possible).

Further analysis: How can LEED encourage increased performance in this area? These credits are extremely important to incentivize, since they are the most directly related to greenhouse emmissions. Should certification level be linked more directly to these credits?

Interesting Findings: Scores in Indoor Environmental Quality have descreased since 2012, EQc4.4 - Low-Emitting Materials (Composite Wood and Agrifiber Products) has contributed most to the decline.

Further analysis: Have costs in this space increased above inflation?

Interesting Findings: Scores in MRc 1.2 - Building Reuse (Maintain Existing Interior Nonstructural Elements), MRc3 - Materials Reuse, and MRc6 - Rapidly Renewable Materials are almost never achieved.

Further analysis: When score points are not achieved, should they be reanalyzed? Do they not apply to all projects (perhaps for MRc3)? Are they too expensive relative to the point total? How could they be incentivized in the next version?

Interesting Findings: Performance on these credits are relatively stable on the NC-2009 version. The dip in years 2012 and 2013 are mostly due to SSc4.1 - Alternative Transportationâ€”Public Transportation Access.

Further analysis: How to encourage completion of SSc5.1 - Site Development (Protect or Restore Habitat) and SSc3 - Brownfield Redevelopment.

Interesting Findings: Scores in WEc1 - Water Efficient Landscaping and WEc2 - Innovative Wastewater Technologies have experienced declines, even as water use and drought has been a major policy concern.

Further analysis: How can LEED encourage increased performance in this area? Can LEED take the lead in pushing for new technologies and adoption of water efficient techniques? If WEc2 is rarely achieved, how can this point be changed or encouraged. Is the cost for achieving this credit too expensive?

Interesting Findings: Scores are relatively stable in these categories. Almost every project gets a credit for using a LEED Certified professional (IDc2). Perhaps this credit is unnecessary, since having a LEED professional seems to be essentially required to achieve certification?

Further analysis: How are the other Innovation in Design credits awarded? Is there variablity across projects or do most score near the average? What is the average completion of the different regional priority credits?
